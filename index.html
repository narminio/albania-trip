Tirana
Energy and one real night out
Blloku is legitimately fun — LGBTQ+-friendly nightlife, the best traditional restaurant in the country, a Cold War bunker museum, and the kind of café culture that makes you slow down. Two nights is the right amount.
Berat
Stone streets, golden light, wine
A UNESCO old town where Ottoman houses stack up a hillside, people actually live inside a medieval castle, and a winery produces genuinely great wine 15 minutes from your guesthouse. It's the romantic middle of the trip.
Himarë
The best Riviera base
Well-positioned for everything on the Albanian Riviera. Dhërmi 26 minutes north. Porto Palermo 13 minutes south. Gjipe Beach 33 minutes. Excellent seafood on the harbor. Three nights here is the trip's heartbeat.
Ksamil
The prettiest-water finale
White sand, three swimmable islands, and water color that looks wrong in photos because it's too blue. Worth the slightly earlier departure morning. One night — come for the evening, leave for the airport.

Climb to Berat's inhabited medieval castle in the late afternoon. Watch the light turn the thousand windows gold from above. Then descend slowly for wine at Çobo and dinner by candlelight at Antigonea.

Osumi Canyon
25 km of towering limestone gorge. Cliff jumps, natural water slides, rappelling through one of the most dramatic canyons in the Balkans. Wetsuits and gear provided. This is the trip's adrenaline anchor.

Thursday, Apr 16 · Himarë
Gjipe Hike-In Beach
33 minutes north. Hike 40 minutes down through a limestone canyon to a completely hidden pebble beach. Crystal water, towering cliffs, almost no one else. The kind of place you don't believe exists until you're standing in it.

Blue Eye spring in the morning — electric blue water from an underground river with no known bottom. Then Ksamil: white sand, three islands you can swim to, and a seafood dinner watching the sun go down on the Ionian.

What This Trip Does Best
The Food

"Albania's edge here is not polished luxury. It's absurdly good value on fresh seafood, dramatic scenery, and long meals in places that still feel completely under-hyped."

The Best Dinner in Albania
Mullixhiu, Tirana. Michelin-trained chef Bledar Kola cooking heirloom Albanian ingredients most people have never heard of. Outstanding wine list. Budget ~€20–25/pp — extraordinarily cheap for what it is.
↗ Google Maps · Reserve ahead
Winery Lunch in Berat
Çobo Winery, 15 min from town. Terrace tasting of 4–5 wines — their Shesh i Zi red is genuinely excellent. Cheese, bread, and local meats included. This is the slow luxurious afternoon in the middle of the trip.
↗ Google Maps · +355 69 206 2726
Riviera Seafood
Three nights in Himarë. Order what came in that morning — grilled sea bass, bream, mussels in wine, cold Korça beer. Taverna Lefteri on the harbor is the reliable anchor. Budget €10–16/pp.
↗ Taverna Lefteri
Traditional Albanian — Berat
Mangalemi Restaurant for arrival night: stuffed peppers, grilled meats, local wine below the castle. Antigonea the second night: stone walls, candlelit, intimate. Two different restaurants, two entirely different meals.
↗ Mangalemi · ↗ Antigonea
Beach Bar Lunch
Day 7 at Gjipe — simple grilled food and cold drinks at the beach bar after the canyon hike. Day 8 in Ksamil — long lazy harbor lunch if the weather is cool. These are the meals that make a trip.
Budget €8–12/pp · Cash preferred
Arrival Dinner · Tirana
Oda Restaurant for Nick's first solo night: a beautiful old Albanian house with courtyard seating, traditional food, warm atmosphere. The right introduction to the country at the right price.

Bunk'Art 2 — the communist secret police bunker
Sheshi Avni Rustemi · 4.5★. Built to shelter government ministers from nuclear attack, now an extraordinary museum of the communist secret police. Surveillance files, interrogation cells, propaganda. Unsettling and fascinating. 1.5 hrs. Pre-book at bunkart.al.

Saturday Night Rules (Apr 11)
  • Do not arrive before 10 PM. Non-negotiable.
  • Order: Radio Bar → Stereo Club
  • Stereo: LGBTQ+-friendly, mixed, safe, goes until 5 AM
  • Cocktails €4–6. Albania is cheap even at bars.
  • Bolt app for taxis — cheap, reliable, no haggling
  • Smoking indoors is normal. Not California.
Money + Practical
  • Currency: Lek. 1 EUR ≈ 105 ALL. EUR accepted widely.
  • Cash essential at Gjipe, Llogara, small restaurants, furgons
  • ATMs in every city. Stock up before going south.
  • Tip 10% — significant in this economy
  • Bolt for taxis in Tirana
  • Albanian SIM card: ~€5 at TIA. Works all trip.
Pack These
  • Real shoes for Gjipe canyon hike + Berat castle

  • Sunscreen — April Adriatic is deceptively strong
  • One going-out outfit for Saturday Blloku
  • Warm layer — April evenings on the coast are cool

Albania Basics
  • Albanians nod for "no," shake for "yes" — this will confuse you
  • English widely spoken under 40 in cities
  • Google Translate camera mode works on menus
  • Accept raki or coffee when offered — hospitality culture is real
  • April = low season. No crowds, best prices of the year.
  • Albania is safe and warm. One of the friendliest countries in Europe.
